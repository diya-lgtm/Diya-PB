Post 01 — The AI agents angle

People keep asking me what changes in fraud when AI agents start doing the shopping.

Here is the simple version. Today, fraud has a human in the loop. Tomorrow, an agent reads the policy, tests the edge cases, and optimizes the abuse pattern overnight. By morning it has run a thousand experiments your fraud team has never seen.

The gap between when an abuse pattern emerges and when it scales is collapsing from weeks to hours.

If your fraud system relies on manual rules and quarterly policy reviews, you are not going to keep up. The companies that survive the next two years will be the ones that move from rule-based to behavior-based detection.

There is no other option. I'm talking about this at SXSW this year.

Post 02 — The COO operator take

Almost no one writes about what the COO does inside an early stage startup. The CEO does press. The CTO ships product. The COO holds everything together with duct tape.

A few things I learned the hard way:

You will spend more time on hiring than anything else. Your first 10 hires set the ceiling for everything that comes after.

You will say no to good customers because they are wrong for the stage. This will feel terrible. Do it anyway.

You will be the boring one in every founder photo. The CEO has the story. The CTO has the demo. You have the spreadsheet. The spreadsheet keeps the company alive.

If you are a COO at an early stage company: post more. Founders don't see your work. They should.

Post 03 — The PayPal fraud playbook

The fraud playbook at large platforms is 20 years ahead of the fraud playbook at most retailers. Not because retailers are slow. Because the talent never crossed over.

When I worked at PayPal, my entire job was looking at first-party fraud at scale. Same name on the card. Same address. Real customer. Behaving badly.

Retail had the same problem the whole time. No one moved over to solve it.

Now that is changing. The Pinch team is built from PayPal, Google, and Simility risk veterans. This matters because retail fraud is not a chatbot problem. It is a behavioral graph problem.

If your fraud team is mostly ops people running rules in a dashboard, that is not enough anymore.
