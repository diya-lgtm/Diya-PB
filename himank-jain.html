The math for scaling ads in 2026 just changed.

200 new creatives per month minimum.

That is the new floor for keeping a 4 ROAS on Meta. For TikTok it is worse.

Creative fatigue sets in at 7-10 days. Top brands are testing 10,000 variations a week.

Almost no growth team I talk to is set up for this.

Most are still producing 20-30 ads a month and wondering why CPAs keep climbing.

The problem is not budget. The problem is that creative production was built for an older internet.

You wrote a brief, briefed an agency, got something back in two weeks, ran it for three months. That entire workflow is dead.

We built GetCrux to solve the part agencies cannot.

Not making the ads, understanding which 1% of the ads you already ran are carrying 80% of your spend.

And then telling you what about them is working.

Most teams think they know.

The data says something different almost every time.

We crossed $1M ARR in months.

Worked with SoFi, Rocket Money, Rappi. Backed by YC, Emergent Ventures, senior people from Google and Facebook.

Total raised: $2.6M.

Most of the LinkedIn version of this story would be celebration posts. Here is what the work actually looks like.

We pivoted hard from a BI copilot to an ad creative platform.

We had to.

The BI copilot space got crowded fast and our wedge was not unique enough.

We took two months of pain admitting the first idea was wrong.

The next two months we rebuilt. We lost some early customers.

The hard part of YC is not getting in. It is being honest enough with yourself to pivot when the data is screaming and your ego is whispering.

We pivoted.

The numbers say it was the right call.

There is a moment every Indian founder in the US has.

You walk into a SF meeting and someone makes a joke about IIT or how Indian founders are everywhere now.

Then you walk out and remember the part that does not get said.

The reason Indian founders ship is not pedigree. It is that we were trained from age 15 to operate under impossible constraints.

That kind of operating muscle does not show up on a resume.

It shows up the third time your product breaks in production and you are still shipping that night.

Pedigree is overrated.

Operating under pressure is the whole game.
